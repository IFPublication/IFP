What is IF Law?

IF Law is a branch of IF Publication designed to amplify diverse voices in legal scholarship, civic education, advocacy, and legal storytelling. It is a collaborative space for aspiring lawyers, legal professionals, community organizers, students, and everyday citizens to write, question, and reimagine justice.

Who Can Contribute?

  • Law students and pre-law students
  • Attorneys, paralegals, and legal scholars
  • Professors and educators
  • Community organizers and legal aid workers
  • Anyone passionate about justice, rights, or reform

What You Can Publish

  • Legal explainers for everyday people
  • Personal stories from law school, the courtroom, or the community
  • Opinion pieces on legal ethics, rights, or policy
  • Case breakdowns or historical legal analyses
  • Know-your-rights guides or resource toolkits
  • Mock briefs, argument samples, or moot court reflections
  • Creative writing inspired by justice (essays, poetry, fiction)
  • Calls to action or letters to the legal system

How It Works

  1. Submit your work via guided templates or upload it directly
  2. Join discussions, leave feedback, and network with others
  3. Get published on IF Law, searchable and tagged by topic

Goals of IF Law

  • Make legal thinking accessible
  • Center voices historically left out of the legal conversation
  • Promote public legal education and reform
  • Build bridges between law and lived experience
  • Encourage critical thinking, advocacy, and creative change
