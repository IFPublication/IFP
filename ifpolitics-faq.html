What is IF Politics?

IF Politics is a branch of IF Publication for exploring political theory, civic engagement, activism, and systems of power. It’s a space for thoughtful debate, personal political reflection, and radical imagination about the future of governance, justice, and democracy. Open to all political traditions and experiences.

Who Can Contribute?

  • Political science students and scholars
  • Journalists, activists, and campaign workers
  • Community organizers and civic educators
  • Public officials, policy professionals, and service members
  • Everyday citizens with political stories or perspectives

What You Can Publish

  • Opinion essays and political analysis
  • Policy breakdowns and legislative explainers
  • Personal stories of civic engagement
  • Interviews with leaders or organizers
  • Letters to officials, open letters, or manifestos
  • Creative writing about politics (fiction, poetry, satire)
  • Community proposals and grassroots ideas
  • Critical thought on democracy, media, or activism

How It Works

  1. Submit your work through guided templates or direct upload
  2. Join political discussions, leave comments, and engage in dialogue
  3. Your work gets published on IF Politics—searchable and tagged by topic

Goals of IF Politics

  • Promote civic literacy and thoughtful participation
  • Amplify underrepresented political voices and visions
  • Encourage respectful, critical public discourse
  • Support democratic engagement and grassroots innovation
  • Bridge theory, policy, and real-life experience
