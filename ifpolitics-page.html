What is IF Politics?

IF Politics is a branch of IF Publication for exploring political theory, civic engagement, activism, and governance. It welcomes nuanced debate, personal political reflections, and radical imagination about the future of democracy and power. Open to thinkers from all political traditions.

Learn More

Who Can Contribute?

  • Political science students and scholars
  • Journalists, activists, and campaigners
  • Community organizers and public servants
  • Educators and civic educators
  • Everyday people with lived political insights

What You Can Publish

  • Opinion essays and civic critiques
  • Analyses of elections, policy, or legislation
  • Political storytelling or lived experience
  • Explainers on voting, policy, and lawmaking
  • Letters to leaders, calls to action, or petitions
  • Interviews with change-makers and movement leaders
  • Creative political fiction, poetry, or manifestos
  • Community-based participatory proposals

How It Works

  1. Create a free account
  2. Submit your work via templates or upload it directly
  3. Join political discussions and reply to others
  4. Get featured on IF Politics with tags and search visibility

Goals of IF Politics

  • Foster civic learning and participation
  • Amplify underrepresented political voices
  • Encourage respectful, critical dialogue
  • Support grassroots visions for the future
  • Bridge theory, policy, and public experience
